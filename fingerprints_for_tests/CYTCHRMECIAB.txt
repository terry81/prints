gd; Flavoprotein pyridine nucleotide cytochrome reductases [1] (FPNCR) catalyse
gd; the interchange of reducing equivalents between one-electron carriers and
gd; the two-electron-carrying nicotinamide dinucleotides. The enzymes include
gd; ferredoxin:NADP+ reductases (FNR); plant and fungal NAD(P)H:nitrate
gd; reductases; NADH:cytochrome b5 reductases; NADPH:P450 reductases;
gd; NADPH:sulfite reductases; nitric oxide synthases; phthalate dioxygenase 
gd; reductase; and various other flavoproteins.
gd;
gd; Dioxygen can be incorporated directly into organic compounds in reactions
gd; catalysed by enzymes termed oxygenases or hydroxylases: oxygenases that
gd; catalyse the incorporation of both atoms of dioxygen into substrates are
gd; known as dioxygenases; those that catalyse the incorporation of only one
gd; atom of dioxygen are termed monooxygenases, or mixed-function oxygenases.
gd; The second atom of dioxygen is reduced to H2O either by the substrates
gd; themselves or by a co-substrate reductant [2].
gd;
gd; Electron transfer components of a number of bacterial multi-component 
gd; aromatic-ring and alkyl-group oxygenases (including phenol hydroxylase [3],
gd; benzoate 1,2-dioxygenase [4], toluate 1,2-dioxygenase [5], xylene 
gd; monooxygenase [6] and methane monooxygenase [7]), belong to family of 
gd; [2Fe-2S] ferredoxin/FPNCR fusion proteins. Other members of the family are 
gd; Yersinia pseudotuberculosis CDP-6-deoxy-3,4-glucoseen reductase [8] and 
gd; S.typhimurium rfbI protein [9]. All contain a [2Fe-2S] ferredoxin domain
gd; fused to the N-terminus of an FPNCR domain. The direction of electron flow
gd; is from flavin to the [2Fe-2S] center. 
gd;
gd; PHEHYDRXLASE is a 6-element fingerprint that provides a signature for the
gd; phenol hydroxylase family. The fingerprint was derived from an initial
gd; alignment of 7 sequences: the motifs were drawn solely from the FPNCR
gd; domain - motifs 1-3 span the flavin-binding domain, and motifs 4-6 encode
gd; the NAD(H)-binding domain. Motifs 1, 2, 4, 5 and 6 correspond to FPNCR
gd; motifs 1, 2, 4, 5 and 8, respectively. Motif 2 appears to be involved in
gd; binding the isoalloxazine ring of FAD; motif 4 includes residues involved
gd; in binding the diphosphate group of the pyridine nucleotide; and motif 6
gd; contains a conserved Cys residue that may interact with NAD(H) and is
gd; necessary for efficient electron transfer [10]. Two iterations on OWL26.2
gd; were required to reach convergence, at which point a true set comprising 11
gd; sequences was identified. Several partial matches were also found:
gd; reductase components of Pseudomonas putida naphthalene dioxygenase (JN0640,
gd; JN0642) and polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon dioxygenase (A55217) lack motif
gd; 2; and Pseudomonas pickettii toluene-3-monooxygenase reductase (PPU04052)
gd; and Vibrio alginolyticus Na+-translocating NADH-quinone reductase 
gd; (VIBNQR363) lack motif 5. Surprisingly, the mmoC component of Methylosinus 
gd; trichosporium methane monooxygenase (C48360), which is functionally 
gd; identical to the mmoC protein of Methylococcus capsulatus (MEMC_METCA), 
gd; lacks motifs 5 and 6 (motif 6 is well conserved among most FPNCRs).
gd; Pseudomonas cepacia 2-halobenzoate 1,2-dioxygenase reductase (PCCBDABC3)
gd; lacks motifs 2 and 5; Pseudomonas mendocina toluene-4-monooxygenase
gd; component tmoF (A47016) and Pseudomonas pseudoalcaligenes phenoxybenzoate
gd; dioxygenase pobB (S44172) lack motifs 3 and 5. All these proteins are
gd; [2Fe-2S] ferredoxin/FPNCR fusion enzymes, except S44172, which has a
gd; [2Fe-2S] ferredoxin domain at its C-terminus. 
gd;
gd; An update on SPTR37_9f identified a true set of 19 sequences, and 146
gd; partial matches.
